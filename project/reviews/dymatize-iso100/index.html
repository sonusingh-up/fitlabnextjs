03 THE LIST

What we liked. What we didn't.

+The case for

  • Highest protein density we've assayed in a commercial whey isolate (90.4% protein by mass).
  • Full transparency. No proprietary blends. Every ingredient amount disclosed on label.
  • Three independent certifications — NSF for Sport, Informed Sport, cGMP — all verified by us via re-assay.
  • Heavy metals consistently <0.05ppm across four production lots tested over 18 months.
  • Stevia + monk fruit sweetened. No artificial sweeteners. Pleasant taste in chocolate and vanilla.
  • Mixability is excellent in cold water with a shaker bottle — clumping rare.

−What you should know

  • Expensive on raw price. ~30% premium over mass-market whey at the per-scoop level.
  • The 38g scoop is unusually large. Many users under-fill it; the price-per-scoop reflects a full scoop.
  • Direct site only sells the freshest stock. Amazon stock can be 3–9 months old (still within shelf life, but not optimal).
  • Limited flavor range compared to mass-market. No specialty seasonals.
  • Salted Caramel flavor is over-sweetened relative to others — the only flavor we'd skip.
  • Not for casein-sensitive users — trace casein within isolate spec but present.

08 COMMON QUESTIONS

What readers ask.

Is this product worth the premium over mass-market whey?
For most users training 3+ days/week, yes. On cost-per-clinical-serving (the only metric that adjusts for actual dosing), Transparent Labs is only 12% above category median while delivering top-decile dosing accuracy and certifications. If you're untrained or training <2x/week, mass-market whey is likely sufficient.
Why does the scoop deliver 28g protein when the label says 30g?
Brands round up. The label states 30g protein per 38g scoop. Our independent lab assay over four lots averaged 28.1g protein per 38g scoop (±0.4g). This is within USP and FDA labeling tolerances (≥90% of label) but worth knowing if you're tracking macros precisely.
How does this compare to plant-based protein?
On leucine content per dollar, whey is roughly 2.4× more efficient. On environmental impact, pea/soy isolate has the edge. On amino acid completeness, whey wins by DIAAS but a well-formulated multi-source plant blend (pea + rice + hemp) closes most of the gap. See our Best Plant Protein roundup.
Are there cheaper products that scored well in your rubric?
Dymatize ISO-100 at 9.0 is our cheaper editor's pick. Same isolate process, slightly less protein per scoop (25g vs 28g), and a sucralose-based sweetener system if you don't mind it. Roughly 20% cheaper on a per-clinical-serving basis.
Should I be worried about heavy metals in whey?
Generally no, for tested brands. Heavy metals (lead, cadmium, arsenic, mercury) are present in trace amounts in nearly all dairy due to soil chemistry. The question is whether levels exceed health-relevant thresholds — Prop. 65, FDA action levels, or the USP Class 1 limits. This product is well below all three.
