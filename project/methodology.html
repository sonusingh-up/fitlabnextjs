METHODOLOGY · V3.2 · MAY 2026

How we
actually test.

Every Fitlab review follows the same pipeline. Same procurement (retail). Same lab (Eurofins, ISO-17025). Same rubric (five weighted questions). Same scoring formula. 01 — PIPELINE

From shelf to score, in six steps.

No brand ever sends us a sample. No brand ever sees the score before publication. No brand has any contact with us between procurement and publication, except to answer specific technical questions on the record.

01
Procure
An anonymous buyer purchases the product at full retail from a randomized retailer. We never accept samples, comp products, or pre-release units.
~3 days · $35–$220
02
Catalog
Lot number, expiration, manufacturer code, retail price, retailer, and receipt are catalogued. Two scoops are weighed and bagged for chain-of-custody to lab.
~2 hours · 4 samples
03
Lab assay
Eurofins or Covance, both ISO-17025 accredited, run protein content, amino acid panel, heavy metals (ICP-MS), microbial, and selected ingredient assays.
~14 days · $480/run
04
Score
Two reviewers independently score against the five-question rubric. Disagreements > 0.3 points are arbitrated by a third reviewer.
~6 hours · 2 reviewers
05
Right-of-reply
Manufacturer receives a 72-hour right-of-reply on factual claims before publication. They cannot influence scoring; they can correct errors of fact.
72 hours · manufacturer
06
Publish
Score, lab data, raw CoA, and all author notes are published. CoA PDF is publicly hosted. Re-test date is scheduled 18 months out.

02 — THE RUBRIC · 5 QUESTIONS · 100 POINTS

Five questions. Same five. Every time.

The rubric is intentionally narrow. We do not score on packaging, branding, customer service, or "feel." We score on what's in the bottle and how it relates to what the bottle claims.

Q.01

Ingredient quality

Form, source, bioavailability. Is the ingredient the actual molecule that worked in the clinical trial — or a cheaper analogue that pretends to be? Synthetic vs. natural where it matters (vitamin E), specific isomers (magnesium glycinate vs. oxide), specific salt forms (folate vs. folic acid).

✓ Full pointsMethylfolate (5-MTHF) — the bioactive form, no MTHFR gene barrier.
✗ Low scoreFolic acid — synthetic, requires conversion, masks B12 deficiency in genotypes.
Weight
25%
What we measure
  • Active form on label
  • HPLC/NMR verification
  • Source disclosure
  • Excipient profile
Q.02

Clinical dosing

Does the per-serving dose match the dose that worked in published human trials? Or is the ingredient "pixie-dusted" at 5% of the clinical effect range? Our threshold for "clinical" is the median dose in the meta-analyzed RCT pool for the primary endpoint.

✓ Full pointsL-Citrulline 8.0g — within or above clinical range (6–8g).
✗ Low scoreL-Citrulline 1.5g — pixie dust; no clinical signal at this dose.
Weight
25%
What we measure

  • Lab-assayed actual
  • Cumulative if multi-dose
Q.03

Transparency

Full ingredient amounts disclosed on label, not hidden behind "proprietary blends." Manufacturer named. Certificates of Analysis published per-lot. Marketing claims align with the dose actually in the product. Brand discloses any third-party relationships.

✓ Full pointsPer-lot CoA, manufacturer named, no prop. blends, accurate marketing.
✗ Low score"Energy Matrix 3,200mg" — five ingredients hidden, no CoA, generic mfr.
Weight
20%
What we measure

  • Proprietary blend?
  • Manufacturer named
  • Marketing/dose match
Q.04

Third-party verification

Independent testing by NSF, Informed Sport, USP, or equivalent. We re-test independently and compare our findings to the brand's published CoA. A brand's first CoA mismatch loses points; a second triggers a public recall flag.

✓ Full pointsNSF Certified for Sport + Informed Sport, both verified by us at re-test.
✗ Low score"GMP facility" claim only; no third-party batch testing or banned-subst screening.
Weight
15%
What we measure
  • NSF / IS / USP cert
  • Banned-subst panel
  • Re-assay vs CoA match
  • Facility audit status
Q.05

Value

Cost-per-clinical-serving, not cost-per-scoop. We calculate the cost-per-unit of the dose that produces a clinically meaningful effect, normalized across the category. Value is therefore a function of dosing accuracy, not raw price. A "cheap" supplement at half-dose costs more per clinical serving than a "premium" one at full dose.

✓ Full points$1.86 per 25g clinical-serving of whey isolate — top-decile efficiency.
✗ Low score$0.92 per 25g labeled but 21g assayed = $1.10 per clinical-serving.
Weight
15%
What we measure
  • Cost / clinical serving
  • Category percentile
  • Bulk-pricing adjusted
  • Cost stability 24mo
Scoring formula · v3.2
Score = 0.25·Q1 + 0.25·Q2 + 0.20·Q3 + 0.15·Q4 + 0.15·Q5
where each Q is the average of two reviewers' independent 0–10 scores on the given question. Disagreements >0.3 are arbitrated by a third reviewer. Hard failures (heavy metals above Prop. 65, mislabeling >20%, banned substances) trigger a maximum score cap of 5.0 regardless of weighted average.

03 — LAB PARTNERS

Where we send the samples.

We use two independent third-party labs. Both are ISO-17025 accredited. We rotate samples between labs as a continuous internal validity check.

Eurofins · Madison

ISO-17025 · A2LA #2856 · Primary

Our primary lab partner since 2024. Performs all heavy metals (ICP-MS), protein content (combustion N), amino acid panels (HPLC), and ingredient-specific assays.

Scope: Active ingredients · Heavy metals · Micro

Covance / Labcorp

ISO-17025 · Backup + cross-check

10% of samples are sent to Covance for cross-validation. Any >5% discrepancy between labs triggers a third blind run.

Scope: Cross-validation · Specialty assays

Internal Lab · BK

In-house · Brooklyn, NY · Pre-screen

Visual inspection, label transcription, pH, density, dissolution time. Used to flag obvious red flags before sending to a paid lab; never used for primary scoring.

Scope: Triage · Photography · Receipt
04 — RE-TESTING · CORRECTIONS · ARGUMENTS

How we update, when we're wrong, and what brands can do about it.

When do you re-test?

Every 18 months. Every time a brand reformulates. Every time we receive a credible reader-flagged anomaly. Re-tests use the methodology version current at the time of the re-test, and we publish a versioned change-log on every review page.

What happens when a re-test changes the score?

The new score replaces the old. The old score remains visible in the review's changelog with the date and a note about what changed. We do not retroactively edit prior scores or pretend a downgrade didn't happen.

What if you make a mistake?

We log corrections on the page where the mistake was made, dated, with a description. Major errors (off-by-a-decimal in a dose, wrong ingredient form, mistaken claim about a brand) get a banner at the top of the review until a re-test is run. Substantive corrections from 2024 onward are listed on our corrections page.

Can a brand contest a score?

Yes, on factual grounds only. We accept written appeals with new lab data or corrected information. We do not accept appeals based on tone, branding, or "you should have weighted this differently." All appeals and our responses are published in a public archive.

What's the right-of-reply window?

72 hours before publication. The manufacturer sees factual claims (lab values, dose calculations, ingredient identification) and can flag errors of fact. They cannot see the score. They cannot prevent publication. They can ensure the published review is factually accurate before it goes live.

Have you ever been sued?

Twice. Both cases withdrew before discovery. We carry libel insurance and publish nothing that isn't backed by lab data or documented sources. Brands that don't like the score have better options than litigation — submit better data.
