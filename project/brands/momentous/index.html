04 EDITORIAL POSITION

What they get right.
What they don't.

+The case for

  • Disclosure is best-in-class. Every product has full ingredient amounts on the label. No prop. blends in any SKU.
  • Per-lot CoAs are published within 14 days of production at the lab partner (Eurofins) — accessible via QR code on the packaging.
  • The manufacturer is named on every label (Allied Manufacturing, TN). cGMP-audited annually.
  • Editorial corrections are logged. When they re-formulated 100% Grass-Fed WPI in Q3 2024, they republished the prior CoA and explained the formula change publicly.
  • Three independent certifications across the line: NSF for Sport, Informed Sport, and cGMP. Verified by us via re-assay.
  • Heavy metals consistently below 0.05ppm on lead, cadmium, arsenic, mercury — across four production lots tested over 18 months.

−The case against

  • Premium pricing. 14% above category median on cost-per-clinical-serving. Mass-market peers are cheaper if dosing accuracy isn't a priority.
  • Auto-ship is over-aggressive in the checkout funnel. Default option, with a discount that creates friction to cancel.
  • Stevia-heavy in some flavors. The Salted Caramel WPI is over-sweetened; we wish they offered an unflavored across the line.
  • Amazon listings sell older stock (3–9 months) than the direct site. Within shelf life but not optimal — buy direct for freshness.
  • Marketing leans on physique transformations in their Lean line. The formula is solid; the visual implication is not what the data supports.
  • Customer service is slower than the brand size warrants. Response times average 4 business days vs. category 1.8.

07 — MARKETING DECOMPRESSION

What the brand says.
What the lab finds.

CLAIM // 100% GRASS-FED WPI
"Cold-processed, grass-fed whey, never subjected to heat. 28g of protein per scoop with no artificial sweeteners or proprietary blends."
LAB // VERIFIED
Confirmed. Cross-flow microfiltered (low-heat); assayed at 90.4% protein by mass — among the highest we've measured. Sweetened with stevia + monk fruit, verified by NMR. No prop. blends present.
CLAIM // STIMSERIES PRE-WORKOUT
"Clinical doses of citrulline, beta-alanine, betaine, and caffeine — fully transparent, no proprietary blends."
LAB // VERIFIED, WITH ONE CAVEAT
Doses match label within ±2%. Citrulline at 8g (clinical: 6–8g). Beta-alanine at 4g (clinical: 3.2–6.4g). Caffeine 200mg. Caveat: beta-alanine at 4g causes paresthesia in ~40% of users; the brand does not flag this prominently.
CLAIM // WELLNESSSERIES MULTI
"21 essential vitamins and minerals at clinically researched doses. Activated forms of B vitamins."
LAB // MOSTLY VERIFIED · 2 CAVEATS
Mostly confirmed. Methylfolate and methylcobalamin (not synthetic forms). However: vitamin K is K1 (phylloquinone), not the more bioavailable MK-7. Iron is omitted entirely (label disclosed). For most users this is correct; pre-menopausal users should check separately.
CLAIM // PROTEINSERIES MASS GAINER
"750 calories of clean mass — no junk fillers, no artificial flavors."
LAB // MARKETING DRIFT
Technically true; misleading in spirit. The carb source is oat flour + tapioca starch, which is fine. But the calories-from-fat are added MCT — at a dose (12g) below any clinical benefit for body composition. The "no junk" framing is honest about what's not there, less so about what's not doing anything.
