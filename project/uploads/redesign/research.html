Creatine monohydrate at 3g/day — does the loading phase actually matter?

A re-analysis of muscle PCr saturation studies suggests 28 days at 3g matches a 5-day loading protocol within ±2% on every measured endpoint. Loading is a marketing artifact, not a pharmacological necessity. We include a worked example for athletes with a competition window.

Ashwagandha (KSM-66 vs Sensoril) — the cortisol claim, examined

Both extracts modestly reduce salivary cortisol in stressed subjects at ≥600mg/day for 8+ weeks. KSM-66 has slightly more replication. Effect sizes are smaller than the marketing suggests and the literature is funded almost entirely by manufacturers.

Meta-analysis · BCAAs

BCAAs in resistance training — do they do anything if you eat enough protein?

No. The signal disappears once total daily protein crosses 1.6 g/kg. BCAAs may have utility in fasted training contexts or for athletes hitting calorie deficits. They are not a general-purpose supplement.

Pharmacokinetics · Caffeine

The CYP1A2 polymorphism — should you adjust your pre-workout caffeine?

Slow metabolizers (~50% of the population, depending on ancestry) show elevated peak plasma caffeine and prolonged half-life. Practical recommendation: 100mg ceiling pre-workout if you don't tolerate it. Genotyping is not necessary to figure this out empirically.
